# Chapter 14: The last tail

Source: Richard Bird, *Pearls of Functional Algorithm Design*, pp. 102-111.

## Role In The Course

This pearl centers on the following problem: Use suffix and prefix structure to compute information about the last relevant tail efficiently.

Use it as a source for examples of calculational algorithm design in Haskell: begin with a clear executable specification, expose the cost problem, then transform the specification by laws, representation changes, or divide-and-conquer structure.
