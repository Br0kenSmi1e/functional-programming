# Chapter 2: A surpassing problem

Source: Richard Bird, *Pearls of Functional Algorithm Design*, pp. 7-11.

## Role In The Course

This pearl centers on the following problem: Compute the maximum number of greater elements to the right of any list element.

Use it as a source for examples of calculational algorithm design in Haskell: begin with a clear executable specification, expose the cost problem, then transform the specification by laws, representation changes, or divide-and-conquer structure.

## Core Ideas

- generalising a scalar answer to a table
- divide and conquer with a sorted auxiliary table
- linear merge as the critical join operation
- comparison lower bounds via sorting

## Key Identifiers And Concepts

- `msc`
- `scount`
- `tails`
- `table`
- `join`
- `tcount`

## Definitions, Theorems, And Proof Obligations

The chapter is primarily a program-calculation pearl. It does not present textbook-numbered Definition/Theorem environments in the style of a mathematics text. For weekly materials, treat the named functions, data representations, invariants, and algebraic laws in the derivation as the formal objects to define before use.

Proof obligations to extract when writing a learning sheet:

- State the executable specification and its intended meaning.
- Identify the bottleneck in the direct specification.
- State the invariant or algebraic law that justifies each transformation.
- Explain the final time complexity and the representation choice that makes it possible.
