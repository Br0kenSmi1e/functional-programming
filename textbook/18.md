# Chapter 18: Planning solves the Rush Hour problem

Source: Richard Bird, *Pearls of Functional Algorithm Design*, pp. 136-146.

## Role In The Course

This pearl centers on the following problem: Model a sliding-block puzzle as a search problem and calculate the solver structure.

Use it as a source for examples of calculational algorithm design in Haskell: begin with a clear executable specification, expose the cost problem, then transform the specification by laws, representation changes, or divide-and-conquer structure.
