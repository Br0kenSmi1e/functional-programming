# Chapter 7: Building a tree with minimum height

Source: Richard Bird, *Pearls of Functional Algorithm Design*, pp. 41-49.

## Role In The Course

This pearl centers on the following problem: Construct a tree of minimum height under leaf-order and fringe constraints.

Use it as a source for examples of calculational algorithm design in Haskell: begin with a clear executable specification, expose the cost problem, then transform the specification by laws, representation changes, or divide-and-conquer structure.

## Core Ideas

- bottom-up tree construction
- forests and left spines
- minimum-height objectives
- links to Hu-Tucker and Garsia-Wachs style algorithms
